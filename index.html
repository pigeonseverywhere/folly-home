folly.

two people. one folio.

folly is a shared budget tracker for two people. It doesn't run on a server. It doesn't have a backend. The data lives on your device and in your own (or your budget partner's) Google Drive.

what folly does

You and a budget partner (yes, singular — no three-person budgets yet) each track your own expenses locally. When you're ready to settle up, folly syncs your entries and your partner's entries to a Google Sheet owned by whoever created the budget. folly reads your partner's entries from their tab and your entries from yours, computes who owes whom, records the settlement locally, and that's it.

google account access

folly requests access to the following Google services:

Google Sheets — to create a shared spreadsheet in your Google Drive (if you're the budget creator), and write your expense entries to it during settlement.

Google Drive — to share that spreadsheet with your budget partner so they can read it.

Google Sign-In — to identify you and verify that the right account is accessing the right budget.

folly does not read any existing files in your Drive. It only touches the spreadsheet it creates, or one that has been shared with you by your budget partner.

what folly doesn't do

We do not collect, store, or transmit your data to any server we operate. We have no backend — we cannot see your expenses, your email, or your Google Drive. We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, or any purpose beyond making the app work.

google api limited use disclosure

folly's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use Requirements.
